Research Question (Displayed in Header Requirement)

How did state responses to perceived internal threats during wartime differ in intent, scale, and impact in the Armenian Genocide and the incarceration of Japanese Americans during World War II?

This project compares two wartime cases where governments justified extreme actions using “security” logic. By comparing them, we can see how similar justifications led to very different outcomes—ranging from genocide to mass civil-rights violations—and why unchecked state power during crisis creates long-term harm.

Project Overview

Our investigation focuses on what each event was, how scholars interpret them, and what this teaches us about governance, human rights, and social justice during wartime.
