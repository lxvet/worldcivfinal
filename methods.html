How specific articles were chosen for the analysis

We prioritized credible institutions and reference works that draw from archival evidence and established scholarship.

Article Selection Process

Sources for this project were selected based on scholarly credibility, institutional authority, and direct relevance to the research question. Major reference works and public history institutions—including the U.S. Holocaust Memorial Museum, the National Archives, Densho, and Encyclopaedia Britannica—were prioritized because they draw on archival evidence, peer-reviewed scholarship, and long-standing historical research.

These sources were chosen to ensure factual accuracy, represent mainstream academic interpretations, and reflect ongoing debates surrounding historical memory and accountability.

Examples of core sources used

  • U.S. Holocaust Memorial Museum (Holocaust Encyclopedia)
  • U.S. National Archives (Executive Order 9066 and education resources)
  • Densho (public history encyclopedia and articles)
  • Encyclopaedia Britannica (reference overview)
